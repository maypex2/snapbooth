Our Story

About SnapBooth

We believe everyone deserves a fun, free photo booth experience no app downloads, no account signups, no strings attached.

What is SnapBooth?

SnapBooth is a free, browser-based photo booth that runs entirely on your device. Open the website, allow camera access, and within seconds you can take Korean-style 4-cut photo strips, classic polaroids, single portraits, and animated GIF boomerangs all without installing an app or creating an account.

Everything happens locally in your browser. Your photos are never uploaded to a server, never stored in a database, and never shared with anyone. When you close the tab, everything is gone. The only thing that leaves your device is the PNG or GIF file you choose to download.

Privacy by design. We didn't bolt privacy on as a feature we built the entire app around the principle that your photos belong to you and only you.

Our Mission

Photo booths used to mean expensive rented kiosks at weddings or mall arcades. Then came apps that required sign-ups, watermarked your photos unless you paid, or quietly uploaded your selfies to train AI models. We thought that was wrong.

Our mission is simple: make the joy of photo booth strips accessible to absolutely everyone, for free, with zero compromises on privacy. Whether you're a teenager at a birthday party, a couple on date night, or an office team having fun at a work event SnapBooth should just work, instantly, beautifully.

Why We Built It

The idea came from frustration. Every "free" photo booth app we tried had at least one of these problems:

  • Required creating an account before taking a single photo
  • Added an ugly watermark unless you paid a subscription
  • Uploaded photos to the cloud "for sharing" without a clear opt-out
  • Worked only as a native app, not in the browser
  • Looked dated and clunky

So we built the photo booth we wanted to use ourselves. The rule was: if it can be done in the browser without sending data anywhere, do it in the browser. It turns out the modern Web is powerful enough WebRTC for the camera, Canvas API for processing, gif.js for encoding animations to deliver a genuinely great experience without any backend.

Thirty-plus emoji stickers you can add and reposition anywhere on your strip by dragging with touch or mouse.
